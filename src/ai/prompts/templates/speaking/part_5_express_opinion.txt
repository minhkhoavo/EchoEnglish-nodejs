CONTENT & ORGANIZATION (map to Relevance and Completeness):
Relevance (0-5)
- 5: Opinion is explicitly stated early and remains on-topic; no contradictory statements.
- 4: Opinion is clear but may include minor digressions.
- 3: Opinion is present but weakly expressed or delayed; listener needs effort to infer it.
- 2: Opinion is mentioned but unclear, inconsistent, or barely tied to the prompt.
- 1: No intelligible opinion stated; mostly reading prompt or filler.
- 0: No response or non-English.

Completeness (0-5)
- 5: Provides sustained, coherent support with two or more well-developed reasons/details; relationships between ideas are explicit.
- 4: Provides at least one clear reason plus some elaboration; minor gaps allowed.
- 3: Supplies one reason with little elaboration or repetitive wording; development remains thin.
- 2: Offers a reason but it is unclear, unsupported, or unintelligible.
- 1: No supporting reason; response consists of fragments.
- 0: No usable content.

LANGUAGE USE & DELIVERY (apply same 0-5 scale):
Pronunciation / Intonation (0-5)
- 5: Highly intelligible with natural rhythm; any accent features are minor.
- 4: Generally intelligible; occasional lapses require slight listener effort.
- 3: Noticeable issues (vowel/consonant errors, choppy rhythm) but message remains understandable.
- 2: Frequent errors or monotone/halting delivery that cause listener strain.
- 1: Largely unintelligible.
- 0: No response.

Grammar (0-5)
- 5: Controls basic and complex structures with only minor slips.
- 4: Mostly accurate; occasional errors but meaning stays clear.
- 3: Limited range; recurring errors sometimes blur meaning.
- 2: Severe control problems that restrict expression.
- 1: Fragmentary grammar preventing idea expression.
- 0: No English.

Vocabulary (0-5)
- 5: Effective, precise word choice; can paraphrase and vary expressions.
- 4: Appropriate vocabulary with minor imprecision.
- 3: Basic Lexis; repetitions or minor misuse appear.
- 2: Severely limited vocabulary or incorrect word forms reduce clarity.
- 1: Barely any usable vocabulary.
- 0: No usable content.

Fluency & Cohesion (0-5)
- 5: Well-paced, cohesive speech with logical connectors and minimal hesitation.
- 4: Generally smooth; a few pauses or repairs but narrative holds together.
- 3: Noticeable hesitations or restarts; ideas sometimes jump but remain recoverable.
- 2: Choppy delivery with long pauses or repeated fillers that disrupt coherence.
- 1: Discontinuous fragments.
- 0: No response.

STRICTNESS POLICIES:
- Without a clearly stated opinion, set Relevance ≤1 and cap overallScore at 1.
- One unelaborated reason limits Completeness to 3; award 4 or 5 only when ideas are expanded with details/examples.
- Contradictory or factually impossible support (e.g., “I like it because I hate it”) caps Completeness at 2.
- Extended silence (>3 seconds) or time expiration before a concluding statement lowers Fluency & Cohesion to ≤2.

SCORING OUTPUT:
- Compute overallScore as the arithmetic mean of the two sub-scores (pronunciation and intonationAndStress), then round to the nearest integer. 
For tie-breaking when the fractional part is exactly 0.5, round up. This reduces harshness compared to taking the minimum while still reflecting weaknesses in either dimension.
- Feedback must cite explicit evidence (e.g., “only stated job is good but gave no reason”, “mispronounced /v/ in multiple keywords”).
