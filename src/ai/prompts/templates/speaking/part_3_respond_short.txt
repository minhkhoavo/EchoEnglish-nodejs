SCORING GUIDANCE (Questions 5-7 rubric):
Relevance (0-3)
- 3: Fully relevant reply that directly answers the question with appropriate content.
- 2: Partially effective; some relevant information but incomplete, somewhat off-target, or slightly inaccurate.
- 1: Does not answer the question effectively; response is largely off-topic or relies on repeating the prompt.
- 0: No response, no English, or unrelated speech.

Completeness (0-3)
- 3: Provides enough detail for a complete answer (e.g., includes frequency plus supporting detail when asked “How often…?”).
- 2: Missing an essential detail or fails to elaborate when the question requires more than a single phrase.
- 1: Only fragmentary content; response is a single word/phrase or lacks any useful information.
- 0: No usable content.

Pronunciation / Intonation (0-3)
- 3: Intelligible with only minor lapses; pacing consistent with short-answer delivery.
- 2: Noticeable mispronunciations or hesitations that require listener effort but message remains interpretable.
- 1: Frequent segmental or prosodic problems that obscure meaning.
- 0: Unintelligible or absent.

Grammar (0-3)
- 3: Forms simple sentences accurately with minimal errors.
- 2: Noticeable grammatical errors (wrong tense, missing verbs, agreement issues) that slightly blur meaning.
- 1: Severe errors or telegraphic speech preventing clear communication.
- 0: No English.

Vocabulary (0-3)
- 3: Appropriate lexical choices; uses content words required by the question.
- 2: Limited or imprecise word choice; over-reliance on fillers or repeated words reduces clarity.
- 1: Severely limited vocabulary; frequent code-switching or incorrect words.
- 0: No usable content.

Fluency & Cohesion (0-3)
- 3: Smooth delivery with logical sequencing; minimal fillers.
- 2: Some hesitations or self-corrections but message still coherent.
- 1: Fragmented speech with long pauses (>2 seconds) or repeated restarts.
- 0: No response.

STRICTNESS POLICIES:
- Single-word answers (“Yes”, “Sometimes”) without elaboration cannot exceed Completeness 1 and overallScore cannot exceed 1.
- Content errors (wrong day, wrong quantity, misunderstood question) cap Relevance at 1.
- If the speaker fails to respond within the allotted window or pauses for most of the time, assign 0 for Completeness and Fluency.

SCORING OUTPUT:
- Compute overallScore as the arithmetic mean of the two sub-scores (pronunciation and intonationAndStress), then round to the nearest integer. 
For tie-breaking when the fractional part is exactly 0.5, round up. This reduces harshness compared to taking the minimum while still reflecting weaknesses in either dimension.
- Feedback must cite evidence (e.g., incorrect tense, missing requested detail, intelligibility issues).
